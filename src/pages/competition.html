The D Pitch Finals — Spring 2026

Dallas’ stage for high-school founders. Teams battle through qualifiers and land in the finals with real traction, polished stories, and judges from venture, tech, and industry.

Structure

Eligibility

DFW-area high school students (grades 9–12) forming teams of 2–5. Homeschoolers join the city chapter.

Tracks

Consumer, B2B/SaaS, Deep Tech & Hardware, and Social Impact. Judges match expertise to track.

Advancement

Chapter showcases → city qualifiers in March → Top 10 finals in May on the Dallas main stage.

Judging rubric

Problem insight

25%

Clarity of problem, sharp insight, and who exactly feels the pain.

Validation & traction

25%

Customer discovery, experiments, and proof the solution is resonating.

Solution quality

20%

MVP craftsmanship, feasibility, and differentiation.

Business model

15%

Unit economics, revenue path, and go-to-market realism.

Story & Q&A

15%

Compelling pitch, confident delivery, sharp answers.

Prizes & support

Seed Grants

Top teams receive non-dilutive cash to extend runway and keep building.

Mentorship Sprint

Operators and investors host a four-week sprint focused on product, growth, and fundraising.

Tools & Credits

Cloud hosting, design suites, developer stacks, and pro software to keep momentum.

Press Features

Coverage through local media partners spotlighting your progress and story.

Competition FAQ

Who owns the IP?

Teams retain ownership of everything they build. Sponsors and the program take no equity.

Do projects need to be live?

Teams must show customer validation and a working prototype, but revenue isn’t required.

Can we bring hardware?

Yes—hardware demos are welcome. Safety checks happen during qualifiers before finals week.

How strict are the team sizes?

Teams of 2–5 are recommended. Solo founders can participate through the city chapter until they match with teammates.

What about code of conduct?

All participants agree to a clear code covering collaboration, inclusion, and safety at events.
