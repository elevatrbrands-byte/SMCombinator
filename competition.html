Competition

The D Pitch Finals — Spring 2026

Dallas’ stage for high-school founders. 2–5 person teams. Four tracks. Real judges. Real feedback.

Eligibility

DFW-area high school students (grades 9–12) forming teams of 2–5.

Tracks

Consumer, B2B/SaaS, Deep Tech/Hardware, Social Impact.

Advancement

Chapter showcases → city qualifiers (March) → finals (May).

Judging rubric

Five categories to show you’ve built something real.

Problem clarity & insight25%

Do you understand the problem deeply and prove you’ve spoken to your users?

Validation & traction25%

Interviews, pilots, LOIs, waitlists, or revenue that de-risk the idea.

Solution quality20%

Prototype strength, technical feasibility, and how well the solution solves the problem.

Business model & GTM15%

Unit economics, pricing logic, channels, and how you’ll reach early adopters.

Story & Q&A15%

Narrative, visuals, live demo, and how confidently you handle questions.

Prizes & support

  • Seed grants for top teams
  • Mentorship sprint with operators and investors
  • Workspace credits / product tools
  • Press features via local media partners

Venue & agenda

  • Venue: TBA (central, transit-accessible).
  • Agenda: Expo hall → Top 10 pitches → Judges’ deliberation → Awards.
  • Finals date: May 2026.

Competition FAQ

Quick answers on IP, hardware, and stage logistics.

Teams keep 100% ownership. Bring your own contributor agreements if needed.
Teams must have 2–5 students. Solo builders should join a chapter to match with collaborators.
Approved with safety check. Bring backup video of your demo in case equipment needs a reset.
All teams sign our conduct pledge—professionalism, inclusivity, and safety first.
